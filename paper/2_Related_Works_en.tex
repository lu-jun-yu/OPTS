\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related-work}

\subsection{Search and Policy Projection}

Monte Carlo tree search (MCTS) and UCT provide the basic search mechanism for this line of work~\citep{kocsis2006bandit,browne2012survey}. AlphaGo Zero, AlphaZero, Expert Iteration, and MuZero develop this idea into a self-play paradigm in which search improves the policy and neural networks fit the targets induced by search~\citep{ref_alphagozero,ref_alphazero,ref_exit,ref_muzero}. Gumbel AlphaZero/MuZero and Sampled MuZero further reduce planning costs in large action spaces by searching over sampled action subsets~\citep{danihelka2022policy,hubert2021learning}.

\subsection{Rollout and Policy Gradient}

REINFORCE and the policy gradient theorem establish the basic form of policy-gradient estimation~\citep{williams1992simple,sutton1999policy}, while TRPO, GAE, and PPO improve update stability through trust-region constraints, advantage estimation, and clipped surrogate objectives~\citep{schulman2015trust,ref_gae,ref_ppo}. In LLM training, InstructGPT, DeepSeekMath, DeepSeek-R1, DAPO, REINFORCE++, GPG, and RLOO/ReMax apply PPO/GRPO or critic-free policy gradients to human-preference and verifiable-reward training, showing that policy gradients over chain rollouts can substantially improve alignment and reasoning abilities~\citep{ouyang2022training,ref_grpo,ref_r1,ref_dapo,ref_reinforcepp,ref_gpg,ahmadian2024back,li2023remax}.

\subsection{Search and Policy Gradient}

Several related directions have emerged for combining search-like exploration with LLM reasoning: self-consistency, Tree of Thoughts, RAP, AlphaZero-like tree search, rStar-Math, and verifier/process-supervision methods are mainly used for test-time decoding, reranking, trajectory synthesis, or process-reward modeling~\citep{wang2022self,yao2023tree,hao2023reasoning,ref_alphazerollm,guan2025rstar,cobbe2021training,lightman2024let,wang2024math}. MCTS-DPO and SVPO use MCTS to construct step-level preference or value signals, while TreePO, TreeRL, TreeRPO, Tree-GRPO, SEEA-R1, ARPO, VinePPO, and SPO more directly incorporate tree sampling, segment-/step-level resampling, or intermediate-state credit assignment into policy optimization~\citep{xie2024monte,chen2024step,ref_treepo,ref_treerl,yang2025treerpo,ref_treegrpo,ref_seear1,ref_arpo,kazemnejad2024vineppo,guo2026segment}. These works are closest to ours, but they mostly focus on search-sample generation, preference/process-signal construction, segment-level advantage estimation, or heuristic branching; in contrast, we characterize how on-policy tree trajectories change the gradient measure relative to chain rollouts, and derive branch-corrected policy gradients, TreeGAE, and OTRC.
