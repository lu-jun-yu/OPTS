\begin{abstract}
Search and learning are two general purpose methods that scale with computation. Motivated by this perspective, this paper introduces search into policy-gradient methods and proposes On-policy Parallel Tree Search (OPTS) and Tree Trajectory Policy Optimization (TTPO). TTPO uses branch-correction weights to address the amplified suffix-gradient contribution after branching points in tree trajectories, and introduces Tree-based Generalized Advantage Estimation (TreeGAE) for multi-continuation structures. OPTS derives the On-policy Trajectory Rebranching Criterion (OTRC) from suffix expected improvement for tree search. Experiments show that OPTS-TTPO outperforms DAPO and PPO on LLM mathematical reasoning tasks, improves over majority voting under the same test-time search budget, and achieves stronger overall performance than PPO on Atari-57 and MuJoCo. Further variance-reduction experiments show that OPTS-TTPO reduces policy-gradient estimation variance more effectively than PPO.
\end{abstract}
